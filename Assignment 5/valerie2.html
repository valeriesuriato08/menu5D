History:
- First recorded water bottle started as buffalo bladders
- Bladders of dead animals or animal horns were used to store water
- Started by the Romans to deliver water to cities
- Animal skins were also used to transport water in smaller portions
- Then, clay or mud used to carry water (Pottery) in 5000 BC
Utility vs Aesthetics:
- Water bottles has higher regard to usefulness rather than beauty
- Used to hold liquid, ease transport of liquid from one place to another
- Although, there are many bottle now that are designed for aesthetics
Affordances:
- The shape, size and material all suggest how we are supposed to use it
- Usually in a cylinder shape suggesting people to use hands to wrap around and pick it up
- Small to medium size to fit in our hands or bags and others
- Usually made of plastic so it is easier to see inside
- Hollow inside for filling up
- Grips on the cap for easier twisting to open and close
- Lightweight material suggests use of transport
- Existing lid suggests it can be filled up, transported without spilling
- Affords to store other things that can seem to fit into the space
Constraints:
- Material constraint as size of bottle allows it to hold only a certain amount (volume)
- Size also suggests who can open the bottle, people with smaller hands find it difficult to twist or hold bottle
- Material must be made of nonporous material so it can transport water and is durable long term to hold water for the time span needed to transport
